**TL;DR** — One free-form prompt plus a brand logo, out comes a 4-second clip of a model walking in sneakers branded with that logo. The shipping pipeline decouples logo placement from scene composition and adds a vision-LLM gate that retries compose until the logo reads. Across a 4×4 prompt/seed sweep: **14 / 16 outputs usable**, gate lifts pass-rate from ~63% first-attempt to ~88% final — a **+25 pp retry-delta**.

**Stack:** Python · FLUX-dev · Qwen-Image-Edit-2511 · Wan 2.2 14B I2V · gpt-4o-mini (rewriter + likeness gate) · ComfyUI · OWLv2 — [github.com/eolecvk/strike-the-pose](https://github.com/eolecvk/strike-the-pose)

## What "good" means here

Two axes — both have to be satisfied for a generation to delight:

- **High-quality video.** Time-consistent, cross-frame consistent, no image-quality issues, no hallucinations.
- **Faithful to the request.** Logo reads like the input logo. Shoe design, model, scene, and video aesthetic match the prompt.

The rest of the page walks the three artefacts the pipeline produces — composed product shot, scene seed, video motion — and grades them on those axes. Where things break, you can see the seam.

    <td>Likeness vs input</td>
    <td>✅ Satisfactory on <b>14 / 16</b> generations</td>

    <td>❌ No texture spec; defaults to printed / embroidered</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Arbitrary logo input</td>
    <td>❌ Only tested with the supplied SVG; coloured / complex logos untested</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td rowspan="3"><b>Shoe</b></td>
    <td>Shape · silhouette · colourway</td>
    <td>✅ Reliable; no rejections on shoe appearance</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Material control</td>
    <td>⚠️ Reliable for canvas / leather; suede + complex materials can confuse compose</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Logo placement (outside vs inside)</td>
    <td>❌ Default outside (lateral) works; explicit placement directives ("logo on heel", "inside as well") do not land consistently</td>
  </tr>
</table>

**Insight.** The structural win is *decoupling* logo placement from scene composition. Earlier iterations tried to do both in one shot and the logo was always the first thing to break. Native multi-reference compose (Qwen-Edit) plus a retry gate is what gets us to measurable reliability.

**Insight.** Scene quality "just works" once the product shot is locked. The interesting failure is **chained branding** — putting the logo on a billboard *and* on the shoe simultaneously breaks both. The gate only watches one surface today; multi-surface gating is the next architectural step.

  <tr>
    <td rowspan="4"><b>Video</b></td>
    <td>Cross-frame consistency</td>
    <td>✅ Stable — no identity shift or flicker between frames</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Time coherence</td>
    <td>⚠️ Videos occasionally slow down mid-clip; pacing not fully coherent</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Image quality (sharpness, motion blur)</td>
    <td>✅ Consistently good</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Hallucination</td>
    <td>⚠️ Text overlays slip through (VLM catches obvious subtitle bars, misses small corner overlays ≈ 3% of frame). OCR pre-check pending.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td rowspan="2"><b>Camera</b></td>
    <td>Movement</td>
    <td>❌ Single subtle camera push only — no orbit / dolly / specific paths</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Framing</td>
    <td>⚠️ Camera focuses on shoes + lower body; full-body framing not steerable</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><b>Aesthetic</b></td>
    <td>Style filters (e.g. "90s VHS")</td>
    <td>⚠️ Works in-prompt with logo intact; retries gate text hallucinations. Stronger path: LoRA + video-to-video filter.</td>
  </tr>
</table>

**Insight.** Cross-frame identity is solid; the residual failure modes are *temporal* (mid-clip slowdown) and *textual* (hallucinated subtitles). Wan is a motion model, not a style model — when an aesthetic directive does land, it usually lands in the still-image step and the motion model just carries it forward.

## Where it goes next

A short list, drawn from the dimensions marked ⚠️ / ❌ above:

1. **Multi-surface logo control** — gate the wall, the shoe, and any chained brand surface as separate likeness checks.
2. **Temporal pacing metric** — flag mid-clip slowdowns the same way the likeness gate flags off-likeness logos.
3. **OCR pre-check on frames** — the VLM misses corner text; OCR will not.
4. **Video-to-video stylization** so aesthetic directives actually land at strength.
5. **Camera-movement control** — at minimum, orbit / dolly / static as discrete primitives.
