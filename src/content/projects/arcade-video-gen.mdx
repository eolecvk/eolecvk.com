**TL;DR** — *Free-form text prompt + the provided SVG logo → 4-second video of a model walking in sneakers with the logo legibly preserved. The shipping pipeline (V5) decouples logo placement from scene composition and adds a vision-LLM gate that retries the compose stage until the logo reads. Across a 4-prompt × 4-seed sweep (richer T2I prompt, retry budget 5, MATCH/PARTIAL accepted): **14 / 16 outputs usable**, gate lifts pass-rate from ~63% (first attempt) to ~88% (final) — a **+25 pp retry-delta**.*

**Stack:** Python · FLUX-dev · Qwen-Image-Edit-2511 · Wan 2.2 14B I2V · gpt-4o-mini (rewriter + likeness gate) · ComfyUI · OWLv2

## What I built

![Pipeline architecture](/images/projects/arcade/architecture.svg)

A **stage graph** that turns one free-form prompt into a video where the logo survives:

1. **Rewriter** (`gpt-4o-mini`) — parses the user prompt into stage-specific slots (`shoe.*`, `scene.*`, `motion.*`), baking in the non-negotiable framing rules.
2. **T2I** (FLUX-dev) — plain white sneaker on a clean background, locked to a right-facing side profile.
3. **Compose** (Qwen-Image-Edit-2511, two references) — places the logo onto the shoe's lateral panel as rendered material, not a sticker.
4. **Likeness gate** (`gpt-4o-mini` vision) — scores the compose output `MATCH / PARTIAL / MISMATCH` against the source logo; on `MISMATCH`, retry compose with a fresh seed (≤ N times).
5. **Recolor → Scene** (Qwen-Image-Edit) — apply colorway, then place the shoe on a side-profile walking model.
6. **I2V** (Wan 2.2 14B Lightning) — 4 s @ 16 fps, 1280×720.

The structural win is **decoupling logo placement from scene composition**. Earlier iterations tried to do both in one shot (Sora I2V, paste + inpaint) and the logo was the first thing to break.

## Main failure modes

Each version was ruled out for a specific reason — full forensics in [`FINDINGS.md`](https://github.com/eolecvk/arcade-videogen-demo/blob/main/FINDINGS.md).

- **V1 — Sora I2V with the logo as `input_reference`.** `input_reference` is cross-attention conditioning, not a first-frame anchor; Sora treats the logo as style suggestion and reinvents it.
- **V2 — `gpt-image-1` scene seed → Sora I2V.** Sora honours the *vibe* but regenerates the *shoe*; the logo gets reimagined every clip.
- **V3 — FLUX paste + FLUX-Fill inpaint + Wan I2V.** Logo is pixel-perfect on a flat shoe but reads as a pasted decal once the foot moves.
- **V4 — paste-and-polish on a product shot.** A single denoise scalar can't satisfy *"preserve pixel-accurate vector logo"* AND *"render as natural shoe material"* simultaneously.
- **V5.0 — V4 architecture, no orientation or likeness guards.** Qwen-Edit has a strong right-facing bias; left-facing T2I outputs trigger compose hallucinations on ~30% of seeds.

## Biggest improvement: the likeness gate

The V4 → V5 architectural pivot ("use Qwen-Edit's *native* multi-reference compose instead of paste + inpaint") got the pipeline to *"works most of the time."* The compose-likeness gate got it to **measurable reliability across arbitrary prompts**.

After each compose, a `gpt-4o-mini` vision call scores the output against the source logo. `MISMATCH` retries the stage with a fresh seed (up to N). Every attempt is snapshot to `white_logo_shot_attempt_{N}.png` so failed retries are inspectable.

## Evaluation & reliability

Two layers; the first is the brief's *"binary, quick to evaluate"* gate, the second is forensic.

- **Per-still binary verdict** (`product_logo_eval.json`, ~3 s/run) — gpt-4o-mini MATCH / DISTORTED / MISSING. Live gate at generation time and auto-flag in the dashboard.
- **Per-video coverage** (`eval.json`, ~90 s/run) — OWLv2 sneaker bbox + multi-scale template-match + Hu-moment contour detector. 4-tier output: `clear / faded / distorted / absent`. Used after the fact, not as a gate.

### Headline metrics — 4×4 retry-delta sweep (v2)

4 prompts × 4 seeds. Retry budget 5, T2I prompt enriched with studio-photography lighting cues, gate verdict tier `PARTIAL` accepted (only `MISMATCH` retries). Usable = `MATCH` or `PARTIAL`.

| prompt | usable / 4 | mean attempts | 1st-attempt usable |
|---|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| navy canvas sneakers — misty harbor | **4 / 4** | 1.25 | 3 / 4 |
| cream leather low-top — minimalist studio | **4 / 4** | 1.75 | 3 / 4 |
| all-black runners — wet pavement, neon | **4 / 4** | 2.75 | 3 / 4 |
| olive suede runner — forest trail | **2 / 4** | 3.75 | 1 / 4 |

**Overall:** 14 / 16 outputs usable. First-attempt pass-rate ≈ **63%**; final pass-rate after retries ≈ **88%** — a **+25 pp retry-delta** attributable to the gate. (Baseline v1 sweep — gate only, no T2I enrichment, retry budget 2 — was 9 / 16 usable.)

The headline weakness is the olive-forest cluster: 3 of its 4 seeds need ≥5 retries and 2 never clear. The gate's job there is to *correctly flag* the failure, not invent a pass.

## Known limitations

- **No camera-position control.** Product shot is locked right-facing because Qwen-Edit's compose-stage logo fidelity collapses on rotated inputs. Camera hints route only to the scene stage.
- **No camera-movement or pose control.** Wan I2V's trajectory isn't steerable beyond loose adjectives; the scene model is hard-coded to side-profile walking.
- **Aesthetic filters are weak.** Directives like *"90s VHS"* pass to Wan verbatim but effect strength is inconsistent. A v2v stylization pass after Wan is the right answer.
- **Lateral / medial blindness in eval.** The eval treats every frame the same; on frames where the *inner* shoe panel faces camera (medial-side stride), `absent` is the correct rendering — but the metric reads it as a miss. Eval coverage is therefore biased downward on gait-heavy scenes.

## If I had 1 more day

These are paths I started exploring and didn't ship.

1. **Promote the per-video eval into a hard gate.** Today `eval.json` is forensic. Wiring `coverage_clear ≥ 0.50` AND `longest_clean_run_s ≥ 1.0` AND `coverage_any_defect ≤ 0.10` into the dashboard auto-flag would tighten the regression net.
2. **Effective-resolution and motion-blur metrics per frame.** A logo can be "detected" but unreadable — small-on-screen, motion-blurred, or low-contrast against the background. Add per-frame `effective_logo_px` (logo bbox area at native fps) and a motion-blur score (Laplacian variance on the cropped logo). Surface in the timeline shading alongside the existing tier colours.
3. **Pose-aware lateral / medial split.** Use Halpe-26 foot keypoints to compute a per-foot heading vector, then split `is_absent` into `expected_absent` (medial side facing camera) vs `actionable_absent`. Lifts the floor on the coverage metric for honest scenes.
4. **SAM2-based shoe segmentation** to replace the OWLv2 sneaker bbox in the overlay. The current bbox is steady enough for ROI cropping but too wobbly to *draw* — SAM2 with a single pose-anchor point gives a per-pixel mask that the contour-fit OBB can ride.
5. **Cross-prompt × cross-seed sweep at scale.** 20+ prompts × 5 seeds to characterize the residual MISMATCH rate post-retry and identify systematically-failing prompt families (the olive-suede-forest cluster suggests something).
6. **Video-to-video stylization pass.** So aesthetic directives actually land. Wan is a motion model, not a style model.
